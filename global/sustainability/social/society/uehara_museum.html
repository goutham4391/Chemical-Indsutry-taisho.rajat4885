Uehara Museum

Uehara Museum

Uehara Museum of Art was established in Shimoda, Izu to display the Buddhist art collected by Shokichi and Koeda Uehara and the modern paintings collected by Shoji Uehara, the Emeritus Chairman. The Uehara Museum of Buddhist Art opened in 1983 and the Uehara Museum of Modern Art opened in 2000, before merging and reopening as the Uehara Museum of Art in 2017. The museum's collection includes art from both the East and the West and from many different genres, including Buddha statues, sutras and other Buddhist art from the Heian and Kamakura periods in Japan, and Western paintings including impressionist paintings and modern Japanese paintings.

The museum displays special exhibitions mainly featuring the museum's own collection and researches ancient Buddhist art in Izu. The museum also contributes to the improvement of local culture through its extensive cultural activities including the invitation of specialists to lecture, the holding of workshops and collaborations with schools to improve art education.

Projects for Culture Promotion during Fiscal 2020

  • Held three special exhibitions. A selection of masterpieces from the Uehara Collection was exhibited and a special exhibition of unknown Buddhist art from Izu was held.
  • Collected and preserved art.
  • Lent its collection to exhibitions held at other museums in Japan.
  • Researched seven temples on the Izu Peninsula and cooperated with three research projects.
  • Held Buddha statue sculpture classes, sutra-copying classes, a Buddhist art course, sketching and watercolor painting classes and Japanese-style painting classes for the public.
  • Held seminars on cultural property photography at the museum in collaboration with the Tokyo National Research Institute for Cultural Properties.
  • Educational collaborations with communities and schools (e.g. facilitating classes taught in the museum, visiting schools for classes).
  • Raised cultural awareness through lectures about local Buddha statues.
  • The museum's curator cooperates on technical matters with Mihotoke no Kiseki, a special exhibition held at the Hamamatsu Municipal Museum of Art.
  • A Buddha statue discovered during research by the Uehara Museum was displayed for the first time in a special exhibition of unknown Buddha statues from Yokohama at the Yokohama History Museum. Uehara Museum's curator was involved in this exhibition.
  • Uehara Museum's curator served as a cultural property council member for five different municipalities and was involved in the project compiling a history of Kawazucho.
  • Uehara Museum's curator participated in the group promoting the operations of the Shizuokaken Hakubutukan Kyokai, lectured and was involved in other activities.
